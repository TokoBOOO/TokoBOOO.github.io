Why Donating Books Matters

Discover how passing on your gently used books can light up imaginations, support education, and strengthen communities. Below, explore reasons, benefits, and practical steps to make your donation count.

Learn More

Why Donate Your Books?

Expand Access

Ensure everyone has free access to books, breaking down financial barriers.

Support Education

Provide resources for schools, literacy programs, and lifelong learners.

Eco-Friendly

Reduce waste by keeping books in circulation instead of landfills.

Community Events

Help fund library events like readings, workshops, and cultural fairs.

Benefits of Donating

  • Tax Deductions: Many libraries offer receipts for your tax records.
  • Declutter: Free up space in your home for new adventures.
  • Sharing Culture: Promote diverse voices and genres.
  • Personal Fulfillment: Experience joy from giving back.

How to Prepare Your Books

  • Check condition: no mold, major tears, or water damage.
  • Remove personal notes and bookmarks.
  • Group by genre or audience (kids, teens, adults).
  • Bag or box securely to protect covers and pages.
  • Label containers for easy sorting by library staff.

Donation Steps

  1. Locate a Library: Use online directories or library locators.
  2. Review Policies: Check acceptable materials and hours.
  3. Bring Books: Drop off during designated donation times.
  4. Get Receipt: Ask for donation acknowledgment.
  5. Stay Involved: Volunteer or attend library events.
